Purple Majesty: Remembering a
Minnesota Icon

The Brief

Prince died on a Thursday. Every paper in the country would run Prince coverage. Minnesota’s paper had to own it.

The Approach

By Friday morning we published “Purple Majesty.” Over the next 10 days I art directed a 16-page commemorative section with four unique zoned covers. Commissioned two original paintings from Robert Carter in less than a week. Negotiated rights to a rarely seen 1977 photograph taken when Prince was 19.

The editorial decisions — when to use illustration vs. photography, how to hold the tone of a state’s mourning — those are the work behind the work.

The Impact

Reprints still sell a decade later. The section became the proof of concept for Star Tribune Magazine.

Denied Justice

Investigative · Star Tribune · 2018 · Pulitzer Finalist · SPJ Public Service

The Brief

More than 1,000 sexual assault cases reviewed. 74% never sent to prosecutors. The Star Tribune committed to showing what the system didn’t want seen.

The Approach

My role wasn’t to design it — it was to make the case for why this investigation deserved the visual commitment, then guide the process. The typography-only cover, the portrait approach, the restraint — those came out of iteration. Renée Jones Schneider’s photography gave the survivors dignity the system denied them.

The Impact
