The Letter

I'm a Packers fan.

But more importantly, I'm a football fan.

Randy Moss and I were both rookies in 1998. He went on to torch every defense in the league. I went into journalism. I'm also a former quarterback, which means I read football from the pocket — coverages, leverage, the MIKE you ID in protection, the half-second before the ball has to come out. I know O'Connell is the real deal because his offense builds for the quarterback instead of around him. And I know that whatever happens at the QB position, you protect the guy who is coaching it.

My actual pitch — and it's not the one you'd expect from a cheesehead:

If you want to win a Super Bowl, you keep hiring old Packers. Ask Andy Reid — learned it in Green Bay, then built a dynasty in Kansas City. Ask Seattle, ask San Francisco, ask anyone who's figured out the part of the NFL nobody says out loud: the best creative and operational minds from that division kept leaving and helping somebody else raise a trophy. The question isn't whether you hire a Packers guy. It's whether you hire the right one.

I'm the right one. Thirty years doing a version of this job in journalism: First CCO/VP at the Star Tribune, four Pulitzer-winning teams, $20M in pandemic revenue. Multi-platform, multi-format, executive-level. Currently consulting on AI-native creative operations for regional media.

I live in Woodbury. I was cheering for Favre when he wore purple — and furious when the Saints put a $10,000 bounty on him. The Vikings aren't a brand to me. They're the home team everybody around me already belongs to.

What I've noticed from the outside:

Minnesota sports fans carry a chip on their shoulder that feels a lot like the rest of Minnesota. Same energy that put Bud Grant on the 50-yard line in a golf shirt at -25 wind chill. Same energy that puts people on a frozen lake in February. Same energy that built the Mayo Clinic. Almost three decades without a major title sharpens that — it doesn't dull it. A creative shop that understands that story can tell it the way it deserves to be told.

One more thing we can all agree on, whichever side of the border we grew up on:

The Bears suck.

I'd welcome the conversation.

Derek Simmons
Woodbury, Minnesota
