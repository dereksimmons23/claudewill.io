03 / 15

The Climb

September 11, 2024. Star Tribune annual offsite. About 40 people. I presented an AI strategy for the company. The vendor I'd evaluated was Krista.ai. The math: 30 salespeople, one extra month each per year. 2.5 years of human leverage annually.

A senior executive stood up afterward and said, publicly:

"Looks like we know who's leading the AI Task Force."

When I delivered the R&D lab proposal to the finance manager, his first question was:

"How many salespeople could we cut if AI can do what you're saying it can do?"

October 30, 2024. 23 days after I disclosed mental health issues to HR, my position was eliminated. 20 years at the company.

June 28, 2025. My mother died.

The 14 months since are what this audition is built on.

The CW Standard

Five principles. They govern everything.

  • Truth over comfort.
  • Usefulness over purity.
  • Transparency over reputation.
  • People over systems.
  • Agency over ideology.

Named for my grandfather. Built over 14 months. The constitutional layer under every artifact in this deck.

05 / 15

The Method

None of it is new.
All of it maps.

Every AI capability you might hire for already maps to something a publishing executive knows how to manage. Editorial judgment. Sales prep. Audience research. Trust calibration. Source verification. AI doesn't replace the work; it changes what the work asks of you.

The most dangerous thing AI does isn't hallucinate. It frames choices, based on input and patterns. The Standard above is how I govern the frame.

Artifact · The Porch

A stranger walked into the porch chat widget on a Saturday night in May. No login. No registration. The conversation that followed:

User Should I leave my steady job to go to a startup
CW That's the question, not the problem. What's actually got you thinking about leaving? The job itself, or something else?
User Chance at building something that outlives me, chance to try something new, only live once, current job is fine but monotonous.
CW That's honest. Monotony's a real problem. But I need to separate two things here. One's true — you do only live once. The other you're assuming — that the startup's where you find it...
CW (final exchange) "I tried" is also the story you tell yourself after it doesn't work. Make sure you know what you're actually trying for before you jump.
User Not sure. I'll keep thinking. This was helpful I'll come back later.
13 messages total. Full transcript on file. AI as coach, not chatbot. Zero supervision.

Replace "porch" with "reader question line" or "advertiser intake" or "newsroom resource desk" and the same architecture works inside Adams MultiMedia.

12 / 15

Adams Is Different

At the Star Tribune, the institutional question was: how many salespeople can we cut?

That's AI as eliminator. The institution couldn't get past it.

At Cascadia Daily News I used AI myself to do the work. Eight months. The publisher got outputs. The team didn't get AI literacy.

An Adams role does something different. It teaches AI capacity into 30 newsrooms. Builds the human leverage Krista.ai would have built for 30 salespeople. At chain scale.

The right question, asked by a different kind of room. That's the role that doesn't currently exist on LinkedIn.
