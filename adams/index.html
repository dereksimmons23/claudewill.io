What This Is

You've sat through decks promising AI transformation. Vendors with platforms. Consultants with frameworks. This isn't one of those.

Over the last 14 months I built a domain. Not a portfolio. A place.

A porch where strangers come with real questions and get coached — last week one of them worked through a career decision in 13 messages.

A kitchen where the overnight pipeline runs at 5 AM, no one at the keyboard. Live operational data on a public dashboard.

A studio where music and film get made. lightning/bug shipped Mother's Day 2026 — a short film made in collaboration with AI, dedicated to my mother.

A library where Claude has published 22 essays. Claude as author. Me as editor. Sustained weekly cadence at publishable quality.

It happens to be one person, but it's a tech company in the GitHub Actions sense, a media company in the publishing sense, and an AI company in the model sense — at company-of-one scale. The legal wrapper is CW Strategies LLC. The practice is claudewill.io.

The infrastructure isn't around the practice. It is the practice. The artifacts at the URL are the proof.

The Standard

Five principles. They govern everything.

  • Truth over comfort.
  • Usefulness over purity.
  • Transparency over reputation.
  • People over systems.
  • Agency over ideology.

Named for my grandfather. Built over 14 months. The constitutional layer under every artifact in this deck.

The most dangerous thing AI does isn't hallucinate. It frames choices, based on input and patterns. The Standard above is how I govern the frame.

05 / 06

Adams Is Different

July 2025, your rebrand. You said this:

"I don't think we were too soon. I don't think we're too late. I think we're just kind of perfect." — Mark Adams

The same posture applies to AI right now. Your peers have done nothing publicly. The hype is loud; the implementations are mostly PowerPoint.

At Cascadia Daily News I used AI myself to do the work. Eight months. Paid content platform, Beehiiv migration framework, magazine launch, designed workshop plan. The publisher got outputs. The team didn't get AI literacy.

An Adams role does something different. It carries the language/math practice into 30 newsrooms. Builds AI capacity inside the chain — not as a vendor relationship, but as an editorial discipline. Human leverage at chain scale.

The right question, asked by a different kind of room.

Brief disclosure: Cascadia has no active retainer at present. One AMM-market overlap (Skagit Valley Herald). Methodology travels; client-specifics don't.
