claude will

A farmer’s name. Claude William Simmons. Born 1903, Oklahoma Territory. Raised eleven kids through dust bowls and depressions. Buttoned his cuffs and walked to the barn the morning he died. Never met his grandson. Never met a computer.

A future tense. Claude will listen. Claude will speak directly. Claude will find a way. Claude will keep going. The verb is unfinished on purpose.

A question about agency. Does the machine have will? The domain holds the tension. It doesn’t need to resolve it. The farmer left a standard. The model runs on it. The grandson built the porch where they meet.

Anthropic’s Claude is the north star — navigational, massive, obvious. This is the little star. Asteriskos. The one you have to look for. A porch light, not the sun. It doesn’t need to exist. It’s here anyway.
