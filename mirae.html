What She Does

There's a gold "M" floating in the corner of most pages on claudewill.io. That's Mirae.

If CW is the grandfather on the porch — the one you sit with when you're stuck, or carrying something, or need a straight answer — Mirae is the person who knows the building. She knows what's on each page, where to find things, and when to point you somewhere else.

Ask her what the CW Standard is. She'll tell you in two sentences and link you to the full page. Ask her who Derek is. Same thing. Ask her something personal — something you're working through — and she'll send you to the porch. "That's a porch conversation. CW's on the homepage."

She handles the quick stuff so CW doesn't have to. Navigation, context, orientation. If you know what you're looking for, Mirae gets you there. If you don't know what you're looking for, CW helps you figure it out.

How She Works

Most of what Mirae does doesn't even hit the API. Common questions — who is CW, what's the standard, how do I work with Derek — are answered instantly from scripted responses. No cost, no latency. She only calls the AI when someone asks something she hasn't heard before.

Hybrid intelligence. Scripted where it's predictable, live where it matters.

Where She Is

Not on the homepage. That's CW's porch. Mirae knows the difference.

The Name

Mirae means "future" in Korean. 미래.

The real version is that Mirae was going to be a daughter's name. Sheri's mom is Donna Rae. The plan was Mirae Jane — MJ — an homage to Donna's middle name wrapped in a word that means what comes next.

Mirae Jane never arrived.

CW is named for someone who came before — a grandfather who died five and a half years before Derek was born. Mirae is named for someone who never came at all. The site is built around family that couldn't be met. One from the past, one from a future that didn't happen.

We put them on things that matter.
