Agreement

1. Engagement

Cascadia Newspaper Company, LLC ("Company") engages CW Strategies LLC ("Consultant") to provide consulting services as described in the attached Exhibit A (the "Services"). Consultant accepts such engagement. The Company acknowledges that Consultant's services are not exclusive and that Consultant may provide services to other clients.

2. Term

This Agreement is effective as of April 1, 2026 and continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated by either party in accordance with Section 8. No services will be provided and no compensation will be due during the month of December.

3. Compensation

The Company will pay Consultant a fixed fee of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000) per month for services provided under this Agreement. Compensation will be paid electronically on the last business day of each month.

4. Expense Reimbursement

The Company will reimburse Consultant for all reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of services under this Agreement, provided such expenses have been preapproved by the Publisher. Consultant will not be entitled to any employee benefits.

5. Company Contact

Consultant's primary contact with the Company will be Cynthia L. Pope, Publisher.

6. Attention and Effort

Consultant will devote reasonable time and effort to the Services and will skillfully serve the Company's interests during the term of this Agreement. The Company acknowledges that Consultant is not exclusive to the Company and may pursue other projects or services independently.

7. Limitations on Authority

Unless the Company has given Consultant specific prior written authority, Consultant may not:

  1. Enter into any contract binding on the Company;
  2. Retain attorneys, consultants, or other persons on behalf of the Company;
  3. Pledge the credit of the Company;
  4. Waive, release, compromise, or settle any claim held by or against the Company; or
  5. Incur expenditures beyond reasonable out-of-pocket expenses reimbursed under Section 4.

8. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement by providing 30 days written notice to the other party. Upon termination, the Company will pay Consultant for all services rendered through the effective date of termination. Consultant will provide reasonable transition support during the notice period.

9. Confidentiality

9.1 Confidential Information

Confidential Information means all proprietary information of the Company which derives independent economic value from its secrecy, including research data, business plans, methods, techniques, and processes disclosed to or developed by Consultant during this engagement. Confidential Information does not include information that: (i) is publicly known at the time of disclosure; (ii) becomes publicly known through no fault of Consultant; or (iii) Consultant is required to disclose by law.

9.2 Non-Disclosure

Consultant agrees not to disclose or use any Confidential Information, during or after this engagement, except as required by the performance of services.

9.3 Third-Party Obligations

Consultant will not disclose to the Company any information subject to third-party confidentiality obligations. Consultant warrants that all work product created under this Agreement is original and will not infringe any proprietary rights of third parties.

9.4 Work Product

Work product produced under this Agreement is the property of the Company.

9.5 Survival

The provisions of this Section 9 survive termination of this Agreement.

10. Independent Contractor

The relationship between Consultant and the Company is that of independent contractors. Neither party's employees, officers, or agents are to be considered employees, officers, or agents of the other.

11. Assignment

This Agreement is personal to Consultant and may not be assigned without the Company's written consent.

14. Governing Law

This Agreement will be governed by the laws of the state of Washington. Venue for all disputes will be a court of competent jurisdiction in Whatcom County, Washington.

15. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings with respect to the subject matter hereof. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts. Electronic signatures are binding.
