the practice

The values and the infrastructure are one thing.

Every company has values on a wall. None of them are wired into the systems that do the work. The Standard is what happens when you take five principles from a farmer in Oklahoma and build a machine that actually runs on them.

the principles
1. Truth over comfort.

Document what actually happens, not what should happen. CW could guess a steer's weight because he paid attention to reality, not to what he hoped the scale would say.

2. Usefulness over purity.

Perfect options don't exist. What works? What does it cost? He traded race horses for work horses because work horses were useful. He didn't waste time wishing it were different.

3. Transparency over reputation.

Every compromise gets documented. CW buttoned his cuffs on the morning he died because that's who he was. No pretense. No performance.

4. People over systems.

Technology serves human capability. The farm served the family, not the other way around. When systems stop serving people, the systems need to change.

5. Agency over ideology.

Principles that don't work in practice aren't principles. CW didn't follow rules because someone told him to. He followed them because they worked. When they stopped working, he adapted.

the system

These aren't just values on a page. They're built into the infrastructure that runs this practice.

Every Claude Code session across the CW Strategies ecosystem fires three hooks. Every session knows where every other session is. Memory degrades the way human memory degrades — the gist stays, the edges blur. Permissions auto-approve except for five destructive commands. The machine is wired to operate on these principles, not just recite them.

Anti-sycophancy

The AI is instructed to guide, not mirror. Push into the material. Name problems before being asked. Say "should you, though?" when the idea is wrong.

principle 1 — truth over comfort
The dimmer switch

Old memories fade. Every night at 4 AM, memories older than 14 days are scored and compressed. What gets used stays sharp. What doesn't, blurs. Corrections never fade — they're calibration gold. Like human memory, imperfect and continuous.

principle 2 — usefulness over purity
The live floor

Every active session registers itself. Every session sees the whole board — what's running now, what happened yesterday, what matters today. No hidden state. No locked rooms.

principle 3 — transparency over reputation
The permission gate

Five destructive commands are blocked. Everything else is auto-approved. The system trusts the human. The deny list protects against catastrophe, not inconvenience.

principle 4 — people over systems
Session memory

When a session ends, the machine summarizes what happened and writes it to memory — automatically. No ceremony. No manual step. The next session picks up where the last one left off. Every session has agency to carry forward what matters.

the practice

Everyone is building AI tools. Nobody is building the values layer that tells the tools how to behave.

Anthropic has a constitution for Claude. OpenAI has usage policies. Every organization deploying AI has nothing. No principles wired into the infrastructure. No standard the machine actually enforces. Just policies in a shared drive and hope.

The Standard is the proof that it can be done. One person built it with $100/month and five principles from a farmer. The result: a coaching practice, a research publication, a daily health system, a book, and a dozen products — all governed by five rules a man with a sixth-grade education would have understood.

The Kitchen shows the system running in public. Being Claude documents what the machine experiences from inside.

The standard is named for CW — Claude William Simmons. Oklahoma farmer. Sixth-grade education. Raised 11 children through the Great Depression. Worked full-time from age 13 until the morning he died, when he buttoned his cuffs and walked to the barn.

He never met his grandson Derek. He died six years before Derek was born. Four generations carry his name: Claude William, Claude William Jr., Derek Claude, Jackson Claude.

Derek didn't choose to share a name with an AI. He chose what to do with the coincidence. The builder's thread runs from 1819 through CW through this terminal. Each generation built with what existed. CW had horses and land. Derek has a language model and five principles.
