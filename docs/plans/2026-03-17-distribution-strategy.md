## 2. Fastest path to 100 Substack subscribers

The current count is effectively zero. Here is the math and the moves.

### The warm network play (this week, 0-30 subscribers)

Derek has 200+ contacts from 30 years in media. Most of them have never seen Standard Correspondence. The fastest 30 subscribers come from asking directly.

**The email (send Tuesday or Wednesday, personal, from derek@claudewill.io):**

> Subject: I've been writing

> [First name] — I started a weekly series on Substack called Dispatches from Inside the Fire. Six pieces published. It's about what happened after the Star Tribune, what I built with AI, and what I learned. Free. No pitch. If you want to follow along: [link]

> — Derek

That is it. No explanation of what Substack is. No description of the methodology. No CTA beyond the link. Send it to 50 people Derek actually knows. If 60% subscribe, that is 30.

**Who specifically:**
- The Cascadia people (they already know the work)
- Former Star Tribune colleagues who saw what happened
- LA Times people who remember
- Poynter Leadership Academy cohort
- Press Or Media board members
- Basketball coaching network (Dispatch #7 is literally about coaching)

### The cross-post play (weeks 2-4, 30-60 subscribers)

The Loss Function goes to Substack Tuesday. The Disclaimer follows in 10-14 days (late March / early April). The Comprehension Problem after that. Each one is a new surface area for Substack's recommendation algorithm. Free posts get recommended more than paid. The existing plan is right — do not change the sequence or the cadence.

### The HN play (spiky, 10-30 per hit)

Dispatch #6 proved HN drives traffic. Post #7 on Saturday. If it hits, the Substack link in the bio and at the bottom of the piece converts some fraction. HN readers who subscribe are the highest-signal subscribers — they will actually read the pieces.

### The LinkedIn play (slow drip, 20-40 over 4 weeks)

Every LinkedIn post this month should end with or contain a single line: "I write Standard Correspondence on Saturdays. It's free. [link]" Not a CTA block. A sentence. The 8 ready posts over 4 weeks at 2x/week means 8 chances to convert LinkedIn followers to Substack subscribers. LinkedIn has the largest existing audience Derek owns.

### The referral play (multiplier)

Substack has a referral feature. Turn it on. When someone subscribes and shares, the algorithm rewards it. This costs nothing and takes five minutes.

**Timeline to 100:**
- Week 1 (warm email + cross-post + HN): 30-50
- Week 2-3 (cross-posts + LinkedIn drip): 20-30
- Week 4 (Dispatch #8 + continued LinkedIn): 20-30

Aggressive but realistic if the warm email goes out this week. If Derek skips the warm email, cut these numbers in half and add a month.

The application is due today. Win or lose, the story of applying is content. Here is how.

### The LinkedIn post (Wednesday March 19)

Not "I applied to ProPublica." That is a humble-brag with no payload.

Instead, the backstory. The frame established March 16 is the content:

**The spine:** Three eras. Coached designers at the Star Tribune. Coached a newsroom at the LA Times. Coached a machine at claudewill.io. Same method every time: someone brings me something, I tell them what to think about, they go try it, we iterate.

**The Robert Carter angle:** Carter's testimonial is proof the method works across mediums. A testimonial from a colleague who saw the work is worth more than any self-description. Use it. **Do not mention ProPublica by name in the LinkedIn post.** The application is the occasion for the reflection, not the content. If Derek gets the job, that is a different post. If he does not, this post still works.
