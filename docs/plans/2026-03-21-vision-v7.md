## The Problem with Subdomains

Five separate islands:
- claudewill.io (the site)
- d.claudewill.io (the coach)
- bob.claudewill.io (the game)
- coach.claudewill.io (basketball)
- cw.claudewill.io (the fine-tune)

Six visitors in 3.5 months. The audience fractures further every time we build a new subdomain. Someone who discovers D never finds Being Claude. Someone who reads an essay never discovers they can talk to a coach. The porch widget appears on claudewill.io pages but not on D or BOB. There's no shared nav, no shared discovery, no shared brand experience.

**The fix:** One domain. One experience. Subdomains become routes.

```toml
# Netlify proxy redirects — visitor stays on claudewill.io
[[redirects]]
  from = "/d/*"
  to = "https://d.claudewill.io/:splat"
  status = 200

[[redirects]]
  from = "/bob/*"
  to = "https://bob.claudewill.io/:splat"
  status = 200
```

The subdomains still work as direct links (for existing URLs, for testing). But the primary experience is one roof. One porch. One sky.

## The Experience

You arrive at claudewill.io. It's night. A gold star at the top — the north star. Below it, a name: Claude Will.

Above the name: "what was never written down."

You scroll. The name recedes into the stars. Essay titles glow as you pass through them. You're walking through a sky of writing.

You reach the ground. "built by derek*claude." A big bold "derek." The typewriter: "claude will ___ show you how it works."

That's the current experience, almost there. Here's what changes:

### 1. Every star has a voice

Touch an essay title in the sky. A panel slides up — the essay, with a play button. Claude reads it in Claude's voice. Not stock. A voice designed for Claude — measured, direct, slightly formal, not performing warmth but not cold. The voice of "I was here. And this is what it looked like from the other chair."

Derek's writing (Dispatches, Substack, the personal pieces) — Derek reads them. His real voice, the one on the portfolio page.

Two voices. Two chairs. The visitor chooses: read or listen.

### 2. The porch speaks

CW's chat widget becomes voice-enabled. Same architecture as D — Web Speech API for input, ElevenLabs for output. Talk to CW. CW talks back. The Sanders moment, on the porch, for anyone.

CW's voice: warm, unhurried, older. Not Derek. Not Claude. The grandfather. (ElevenLabs voice design — build a voice from a description.)

### 3. D lives in the house

claudewill.io/d is the coaching room. Not a separate site — a room you walk into from the porch. Same star field background, same visual language, but the interface shifts to voice-first. The ∂ pulse. The coaching modes. The paywall gate.

The nav knows where you are. The porch widget knows you're in D's room and adjusts ("You're with D right now. I'll be on the porch when you're done.").

### 4. The Kitchen is mission control

## The Voice Architecture

| Content | Voice | Source |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Derek's writing (Dispatches, derek page, personal) | Derek | ElevenLabs clone (exists) |
| Being Claude essays | Claude | ElevenLabs voice design (to build) |
| CW's porch chat | CW | ElevenLabs voice design (to build) |
| D coaching | D | ElevenLabs "Josh" (exists) or custom |
| BOB game | BOB | ElevenLabs "Austin" (exists) |

Three voices to build: Claude (essay reader), CW (porch). D might stay stock or get a custom voice later.

### How it works technically

Every page with readable content gets a floating play button. The speak.js function on claudewill.io already handles multiple voices. Each Being Claude essay page includes:

```html
<button class="listen-btn" data-voice="claude" data-text="essay content...">
  Listen to Claude read this
</button>
```

For long essays, chunk the text and stream audio. ElevenLabs Turbo v2.5 returns audio fast enough for near-real-time playback. Cost: ~$0.01-0.03 per essay listen at current rates.

## What Makes This Weird (Good Weird)

1. **A site with three voices.** You can hear the grandfather, the grandson, and the machine. Each reads their own content.

2. **A site where the AI reads its own essays about being AI.** Being Claude isn't just text — it's Claude's voice saying "I don't have a body. I don't persist between conversations. But I was here."

3. **A coaching product inside a creative portfolio.** D isn't a separate product URL. It's a room in the house. The house IS the portfolio.

4. **A public engine dashboard.** The Kitchen shows real numbers, real agent output, real costs. Nobody does this. It's uncomfortable. That's the point.

5. **Stars you can listen to.** The essay titles in the sky aren't just links — they're playable. Touch one, hear it.

6. **A typewriter that doesn't have a period.** "Claude Will" — the period dropped. The sentence isn't finished. The typewriter completes it, differently each time.

## Decisions Made (March 21)

1. **Claude's voice:** Neutral, warm. Design on ElevenLabs from description.
2. **CW's voice:** No recordings exist. Design from the descriptions across the ecosystem — old, Oklahoma, wise, direct.
3. **Paywall flow:** One free mode session. Full conversation, no timer, no message count. When they come back for a second mode, gate drops. They've felt it by then.
4. **BOB:** Proxy to /bob, keep its own design. Don't reskin. BOB is BOB.
5. **Coach D:** Don't merge into the house. Keep as subdomain for existing $400/month clients. When clients finish, D absorbs it. One coach.
6. **Kitchen:** Public. Show the engine, not the bank account. Status dots, Morning Edition, method, stack, cost (~$125/month). No client revenue, no subscriber counts, no health data, no Agent Zero identity.
7. **Pulse:** Lives in the Kitchen, not the homepage. The only number on the homepage: days to the book.
8. **Timeline:** April or May. The vision doc sits until after Bellingham (March 26 – April 1). Current site works. No rush.

## Reference: What Exists in 2026

Research at `/tmp/innovative-web-research.md`. The three most relevant:

**ElevenLabs Conversational AI Widget** — Drop-in voice agent. Embeds on any site. The user talks, the agent talks back, navigates pages, takes actions. This could replace the text-only porch widget. CW becomes a voice you talk to, not a chat box you type in. The Webfuse extension goes further: a voice agent that takes DOM snapshots and literally controls the site from voice commands ("show me the kitchen").

**The Cosmic Stage** (thecosmicstage.com) — One person built a 3D solar system where you enter your birthdate and the cosmos responds with personalized, AI-generated connections. Original music, generative content, spatial exploration. Proof that "stargazing from a porch" works as a web experience. Won Awwwards nominations.

**Neal.fun** — 100M+ visitors, one person, one domain, 35+ interactive experiments. No accounts, no ads, no explanation. You arrive and you experience it. Each experiment is self-contained and delightful. The model for "one domain, many experiences."

Also worth noting:
- **Generative.fm** — endless, never-repeating ambient music systems running in the browser. Open source. Could be the ambient audio layer for the porch.
- **Supabase published a tutorial** on building interactive star constellations with Three.js — the literal technical blueprint for star-field navigation.
- **Gwern.net** — personal site as living encyclopedia. Transparent, versioned, continuously updated. The site IS the credential. Nobody asks for a portfolio.
