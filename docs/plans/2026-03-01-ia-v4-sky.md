## Three Open Questions

### 1. Light/Dark Mode

Can the sky work in both modes?

**Dark mode (night sky):** Natural. Bright stars on dark background. Gold asterisks glow. This is the strong version.

**Light mode (dawn sky):** The sky isn't always dark. Light mode = early morning. Warm cream background, gold embers instead of bright white stars. Subtler but same layout. The concept shifts from midnight to 5 AM.

**Current palette:**
- Light: #fafaf8 bg, #0a1628 text, #d4a84b accent
- Dark: inherited from prefers-color-scheme override

**Needs:** Specific light-mode palette for the sky concept. The current warm white might work as a dawn sky — but the stars need to be visible against it. Maybe the light mode stars are deeper gold/amber instead of bright gold.

### 2. House vs. Sky Metaphor

Derek flagged: "those aren't names of stars." Workshop, kitchen, standard — house words, not sky words.

**House pros:**
- Already built into the site language (kitchen, workshop, porch)
- Familiar, intuitive
- "House rules" for The Standard is strong
- The footer "derek*claude" works as both house and sky

**House cons:**
- "Pretty tired concept" (Derek's words)
- Limits the visual language — rooms are boxes, doors are rectangles
- Doesn't match the asterisk/star/light direction
- Mixing metaphors (stars named "kitchen") doesn't work

**Sky pros:**
- Matches the existing symbols (asterisk, porch light, dawn, asteriskos)
- Opens up the visual language — any device, any size, responsive by nature
- The asterisk has always been the most important element on the site
- "Stars are portals" is more interesting than "doors lead to rooms"
- Scales from Apple Watch to 70" TV (points of light, not boxes)

**Sky cons:**
- Needs new names for everything (can't call a star "kitchen")
- More abstract — risk of being too clever
- SEO/accessibility: stars need labels, structure, semantic HTML
- Bigger rebuild than the house approach

**If sky, possible names:**
- "the work" (bio, writing, essays, portfolio, contact) — or just unlabeled, the brightest stars
- "the method" (consulting, proof, pricing, form) — instead of "workshop"
- "the engine" or "the system" (live dashboard) — instead of "kitchen"
- Or: drop metaphorical names entirely. Just describe what's there.

### 3. Do We Need Pages At All?

Derek's question: "the sky is wide and deep. the stars are portals through the domain. we might not need traditional nav at all."

**Full portal model:** Clicking a star opens content within the sky (overlay/panel/zoom). You never leave the homepage. You zoom into points of light. Close → back to the sky.

**Pros:**
- Distinctive. Nobody else has this.
- The sky IS the site. Not a homepage with links to other pages.
- Cohesive experience — no page loads, no navigation jumps.
- Works naturally on any screen size.

**Cons:**
- Long-form content (Being Claude essays, the story) probably needs real URLs for sharing and SEO.
- Browser history / back button needs pushState management.
- Screen readers need clear semantic structure.
- Performance: loading all content into one page could be heavy. Lazy loading needed.
- Forms need dedicated focus (assessment form in an overlay could feel cramped).

**Hybrid option:** The homepage is the sky. Short content (bio, portfolio, form, the standard) opens as portals. Long content (essays, the story, the full kitchen dashboard) has its own URL but matches the sky aesthetic. Best of both — the sky is home, the stars are sometimes portals and sometimes hyperlinks. The user doesn't care about the difference.
