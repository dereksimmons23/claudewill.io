## The Concept

The homepage is a sky. The asterisk is the brightest star. The site is not a house with rooms. It's a sky with points of light.

### March 1 — The Starting Point

Derek's words:
- "claudewill is great and it's the domain and blah blah blah, but it's not home."
- "what if it's just the porch light, the asterisk, the asteriskos"
- "the light isn't just the porch, it's also the sun" (Dawn connection)
- "derek and claude aren't two separate rooms. it's the same room, different stories."
- "menu should be called map" — DONE
- "the standard isn't a room. it's the house rules. it lives in the foundation, not the nav."
- "do we need pages and subpages at all? the sky is wide and deep. the stars are portals through the domain."

### March 2 — The Naming Conversation

Started with four stars (the work, the workshop, the kitchen, the hallway). Evolved through:

1. **"the work" and "the workshop" feel duplicitous.** Same idea, two names.
2. **Entity-based naming:** derek, cw, claude. Name the stars after who lives here, not what they do.
3. **The kitchen feels weird** when the other two are people. Two persons and a room breaks the pattern.
4. **Two stars + asterisk:** derek and claude. But then: "do you really need a star?"
5. **The site IS Claude.** claudewill.io — the domain IS the machine's address. The whole building is Claude. Claude doesn't need a door because the building IS Claude. The asterisk IS Claude.
6. **"The hallway" as the star between the asterisk and derek.** Being Claude IS the hallway (from article #8). The shared space. The evidence of what happens when they work together.

Derek's final words before handing off:
- "do you really need a star? or is it hallway as the star between something and derek?"

## The Emerging Model

```
                    *
                claudewill

          ✧ derek    ✧ the hallway
```

### * — claudewill (IS Claude)

The whole site is the machine's home. The asterisk breathes. Click it → the porch opens (CW chat). The business, the standard, the method — all live in the asterisk. You don't need to navigate to Claude. You're already here.

### ✧ derek — the human

Everything his. Bio, story, career, portfolio (honeycomb grid), writing (Dispatches), contact. /derek becomes the hub. /story folds in. The warm star. The resident who chose to live here.

### ✧ the hallway — the connection

Being Claude articles. Finding Claude (the book). The method documentation. The engine (kitchen). The collaboration evidence. Not a person, not a room — the space where derek and claude meet in public. Every essay, every chapter, every artifact of working together.

The hallway is what you walk through to understand what happens here.

### What's NOT a star

- **The workshop** — CTA destination. Reached through porch conversations, "work with me" buttons, /standard pricing. Not navigation.
- **The kitchen** — Folds into the hallway. The engine IS collaboration evidence. Or stays hidden, discoverable.
- **The standard** — Foundation. Every page footer. Own URL at /standard. Not nav.
- **The story** — Folds into /derek. Derek's story IS Derek's page.
- **The map** — Site index in the footer. Not nav.

## Open Questions (updated March 2)

### 1. Light/Dark Mode — PROTOTYPED

Four palettes at `dev/sky-palette.html`:
- **Night:** #070d1a bg, gold stars on midnight. The strong version.
- **Dawn:** #f5f0e6 bg, warm amber on cream. 5 AM.
- **Current light:** #fafaf8 bg, existing site palette.
- **Current dark:** #0a1628 bg, existing dark mode.

Night is the winner so far. Dawn is interesting but untested with real content.

### 2. "The Hallway" as a Name — OPEN

Pros: Mysterious, invites curiosity, consistent with Being Claude #8 thesis ("Being Claude IS the hallway"), correctly describes the shared space.

Cons: A visitor clicking "the hallway" doesn't know what they'll find. Abstract. Could be confused for a literal hallway.

Alternatives: "being claude" (descriptive but limits scope to just the articles), "the practice" (what they do together), "the work" (back to original problem), just "claude" (but we said the site IS Claude).

### 3. Spatial Arrangement — PROTOTYPED

Three layouts in sky-palette.html (press V to cycle):
- **Default:** Asterisk on top, two stars centered below wordmark
- **Constellation:** Stars flanking the asterisk horizontally
- **Horizon:** Stars pinned to bottom of viewport, asterisk floats above

### 4. Where the Kitchen Goes — OPEN

Options:
a. Folds into the hallway (the engine IS the collaboration story)
b. Stays as a hidden page, discoverable through porch or footer
c. Becomes a section within /derek (Derek built the engine)
d. Dies (Derek said Feb 28: "The Kitchen is pretty but doesn't bring clients")
