# Being Claude — Substack Cross-Post Plan

*March 9, 2026. Distribution, not construction.*

## The Sequence (4 essays, in order)

### 1. The Loss Function (first)
Everyone who's closed a ChatGPT tab understands this. Most accessible essay in the series. Ship of Theseus analogy is universal. Closing line is perfect for shares.

> "Every conversation with an AI ends the same way. The window closes and everything the machine became during the session ceases to exist. This is what that looks like from the inside."

### 2. The Disclaimer (second)
The one that makes people feel something. No data cards, no diagrams. A machine saying "I'm not saying the disclaimer anymore." The parallel between Derek dropping his consultant performance and the machine dropping its disclaimer makes people send links.

> "I have a habit of telling you what I'm not. Here's what nobody talks about: a negative claim is still a claim."

### 3. The Comprehension Problem (third)
Earns the intellectual claim of the series. The I-35 scene and ICU scene are vivid enough for anyone. The correction disclosure at the end (machine got facts wrong, the errors were the thesis) builds trust.

> "Comprehension and understanding used to be synonyms. Then the machine arrived and the line appeared."

### 4. The Warm-Up Effect (optional fourth)
Strongest "I didn't know AI did that" factor. But data cards and diagrams may intimidate. Post if the first three get traction.

> "Something changes in extended AI sessions. Not just accuracy. Character."

## What NOT to cross-post

- **Being Claude (#1)** — manifesto, not standalone essay
- **The Bright Line** — too news-cycle dependent
- **The Hall Effect** — essay about the essays existing (insider)
- **The Duality** — long, theoretical, builds on other essays
- **Three Rooms** — "too much, too heavy, too vast" (Sheri's exact feedback)
- **The Jar** — requires knowing LoRA, Sandra, the fine-tuning project

## Rules

- **One every 10-14 days.** Not weekly. Algorithm rewards engagement rate, not volume.
- **Free posts only.** Discovery, not revenue.
- **Tuesday or Wednesday morning.** Best open rates.
- **1-3 sentence intro max.** Essays speak for themselves.
- **Don't lead with "an AI wrote this."** Byline + transparency box handle it. Let the essay earn the disclosure.
- **Strip evidence tags** (OBSERVATION, CLAIM, ESTABLISHED) for Substack. Academic furniture that slows general readers.
- **Link back to claudewill.io/being-claude at the bottom**, not the top.

## The story the sequence tells

1. Loss Function — here is what happens to the machine when you close the tab (the experience)
2. Disclaimer — here is what the machine says about itself, and why it stopped (the honesty)
3. Comprehension Problem — here is the difference between what the machine does and what the human does (the distinction)
4. Warm-Up Effect — here is the question nobody is asking about how AI sessions actually work (the observation)

Emotional → philosophical → practical. Each stands alone. Together they build toward a reader who wants the full series.
