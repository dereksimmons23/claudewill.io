## What They're Actually Hiring

An **editorial judgment job** — commissioning illustration and photography for investigative stories. Not a design job. The role sits on the visuals team alongside 5 other visuals editors, reporting to Art Director **Lisa Larson-Walker** (formerly Slate, Newsweek/Daily Beast) and Senior Editor **Boyzell Hosey**.

**Core skill:** Commissioning. Finding the right creative talent for an investigation, writing the brief, guiding the vision, negotiating rights.

**What they value:**

1. **Commissioning is the job.** They commission heavily from external artists. Every recent hire came from art direction/photo editing backgrounds.
2. **Empathy is non-negotiable.** "Compassionate" and "empathetic" are the first adjectives in the posting. They tell stories about vulnerable people failed by powerful institutions.
3. **Program-building stands out.** Recent hires: Cengiz Yar built Rest of World's visual program from launch. Andrea Wise co-founded Diversify Photo. Peter DiCampo co-founded Everyday Africa. They hire builders, not executors.
4. **Investigative understanding.** Interpret complex investigations and translate them visually.
5. **Impact is the metric.** ProPublica measures success by legislative change, not pageviews. 2025 Pulitzer for Public Service came from a visuals-first photo essay that led to 12+ bills in 7+ states.
6. **Novel formats welcome.** "Fresh ideas... unbound by precedent."
7. **AI: cautious but engaged.** They use AI for investigation (classifying Uvalde recordings, finding data patterns). They do NOT use AI to generate content.

### Project 2: Prince — The Full Arc (Commissioned — Breaking News)

**The story:** Prince dies on a Wednesday or Thursday, April 2016. By Sunday, Derek's team has published a commemorative section with four zoned covers — a similar approach to the Olympics poster series he did at the LA Times. The breaking news front page included a full-page illustration commissioned and published within hours.

**Why this one:** Demonstrates the CORE skill they're hiring for — commissioning under extreme deadline — plus sustained editorial direction across an entire commemorative product (not just a front page). Shows compassion, not just speed.

**The commissioning decision:** Why illustration over photography? Prince had one of the most photographed faces in music history. Every paper ran a photo. The illustration was an editorial choice — "this is not a news event, this is a loss."

**URLs:**
- Strib Store (reprint set, still selling): https://stribstore.com/products/remembering-a-minnesota-icon-prince-page-poster-reprint-set
- Video walkthrough: https://www.youtube.com/embed/M6tn3Q7i7-c
- Upload PDF of front page + four covers

**Key detail: Illustrator is Robert Carter** (two of four covers). Breaking news front page ("PURPLE MAJESTY") April 22, 2016. 16-page special section May 1, 2016.

**Backstory angles:**
- The editorial judgment: illustration vs. photography for a death. Every paper ran a photo. You commissioned art. "This is not a news event, this is a loss."
- The timeline: Prince dies Thursday → breaking news front page Friday → commemorative section with four covers by Sunday
- The commissioning: Robert Carter for two covers. How fast was the brief? What was the direction?
- The scope: 4 zoned covers, 16-page tribute, cross-team coordination under grief
- The impact: newsstand copies became collector's items (still selling reprints at $29.99-$115). The coverage set the tone for Minnesota's mourning.
- Star Tribune Magazine launched one year later (April 2017) — debut cover was the Prince legacy story. The commemorative section was the proof of concept for the magazine.

**Pattern (mention in backstory, don't dwell):** Prince was one of many — Michael Jackson (LA Times), John Wooden (LA Times), Obama election, California wildfires, Trump Wave. But it was the one where every editorial decision mattered most.
