## What Dawn Is

A daily health accountability practice built around sleep, not weight. Dawn/dusk check-ins. Honest accounting. Partner-based. Evidence-backed. The practice for people fighting Night Eating Syndrome without medication — or alongside it.

Dawn is not a fitness app, not a diet plan, not a chatbot. It is a structured daily practice with clinical framing and referral pathways.

## The Hierarchy

1. **Sleep quality** — the foundation everything else breaks without
2. **The noise** — hormonal (ghrelin, inverted glucose) and social (family jokes, hiding, shame)
3. **The overnight raid** — the symptom, not the disease
4. **The weight** — the scoreboard, not the game

## Why It Exists

15 years of Better Me journals. Same patterns. Same language. Same obstacles. The data shows what works (going dry, 14-16h fasts, daily walks, partner accountability, honest logging) and what doesn't (willpower, aggressive fasting, solo attempts, all-or-nothing thinking).

In 2026, Derek and Sheri started a 151-day challenge (Jan 1 - May 31). Day 61 as of this document. 12.8 lbs down. 61 days unbroken practice. Raids continue almost nightly. The practice works WITH the raids, not by eliminating them.

## The Clinical Picture

### Night Eating Syndrome (NES)

- DSM-5 classified under OSFED (Other Specified Feeding or Eating Disorders)
- 25%+ of daily calories consumed after evening meal AND/OR waking to eat 2+ times/week
- Prevalence: 1-2% general population, ~10% of obese individuals
- Circadian phase delay in eating rhythm (1.0-2.8h), attenuated melatonin and leptin, elevated cortisol, inverted glucose pattern
- **No NES-specific digital tool or app exists** (as of March 2026)
- Only self-help resource: *Overcoming Night Eating Syndrome* (Allison & Stunkard, 2004)

### Evidence-Based Treatments

- **CBT for NES**: Nocturnal ingestions dropped from 8.7 to 2.6/week (pilot study)
- **Sertraline**: 71% responder rate vs 18% placebo (RCT, O'Reardon et al., 2006)
- **Bright light therapy**: 10,000 lux morning light for 2 weeks reduced NES symptoms
- **Implementation intentions**: Medium-to-large effect size across 642 tests. "If I wake at 2am, then I drink water and return to bed" > "I won't eat at night"

### Derek's Co-occurring Conditions

- **Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS)**: 20+ years. Fragments sleep architecture. Triggers waking. Waking triggers ghrelin spike. Ghrelin triggers eating. This may be the root cause, not NES itself.
- **Childhood food patterning**: Food omnipresent growing up. Free access, no shame. Sleepwalking to fridge as a child (SRED-adjacent, not just NES).
- **Separate sleeping**: Has not shared a bed with Sheri in 20 years due to RLS. Overnight eating happens in complete isolation — no witness, no intervention.
- **Medical gap**: Told a doctor about overnight eating ~2015. Doctor asked "why." No sleep study, no NES screening, no RLS co-occurrence evaluation. A decade without proper diagnosis.

### The Medical Cascade

RLS (20 years, undertreated) → fragmented sleep → ghrelin/cortisol disruption → nocturnal eating → degraded sleep quality → mood/energy collapse → family treats it as humor → isolation → hiding → more eating alone at 2am

### Clinical Referral Built Into Product

Dawn is not a substitute for medical care. The practice should explicitly recommend:
- Sleep study (RLS + NES co-occurrence evaluation)
- Sleep specialist consultation
- Discussion of dopamine agonists or gabapentin for RLS
- GLP-1 conversation when appropriate (not anti-medication — pro-agency)

### What Exists

| Product | What It Does | Why It's Not Dawn |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Claude for Healthcare | Reads lab results, health records | Medical data reader, not a practice |
| Noom | CBT-based weight loss coaching, ~$60/mo | General weight loss, not NES-specific |

| Woebot/Wysa/Flourish | Mental health chatbots | Anxiety/depression, not eating disorders |
| MyFitnessPal/Lose It | Calorie tracking | Logging tool, not a practice |
| GLP-1s | Pharmacological appetite suppression | Medication, not behavioral practice |
| Allison/Stunkard workbook | NES self-help (2004) | Book, not digital. 20 years old. |

### The Gap

No NES-specific digital tool exists. No partner-based health practice with periodized rhythm. No product that says "the system is rigged against you, here's how to practice anyway." No product built on 15 years of personal evidence.

### Market Reality

- ~10 million Americans with NES symptoms
- Realistic paying customers for Dawn: hundreds, maybe low thousands
- Coach D scale ($400/month), not Noom scale ($600M/year)
- Value may be greater as proof-of-method for CW Strategies consulting than as standalone product revenue

## What Turns Prose Into Product

Derek's instinct: "stop calling the overnight raid guilt and actually provide resources for help."

### From Journal to Practice

| Journal Version | Product Version |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "It's guilt. Eating when no one is looking." | NES is a clinically recognized condition with measurable hormonal signatures. Here's what's happening in your body at 2am. |
| "Overnight raid ~500 cal" | Structured raid logging with pattern analysis: time, trigger, food type, sleep quality before/after |
| "The 8 questions" | Evidence-backed practice framework with citations |
| "Going dry works" | Alcohol cessation research: liver fat drops 15%, blood sugar falls 23%, sustained consumption decrease at 6 months |
| "5-2-5 rhythm" | Periodized restriction with structured breaks — attenuates metabolic adaptation (peer-reviewed) |
| "Belt compass" | Physical feedback mechanism (evidence-based self-monitoring) |
| "Better Us with Sheri" | Partner accountability protocol (research-validated dyadic health behavior change) |
| "What can you create today?" | Implementation intentions: "If X, then Y" — strongest validated behavior change technique |

### Resources the Product Provides

- NES self-screening (based on Allison diagnostic criteria)
- When to see a sleep specialist (RLS co-occurrence, SRED vs NES)
- When to discuss GLP-1s (not anti-medication, pro-informed-choice)
- CBT techniques for nocturnal eating cognitions
- Bright light therapy protocol (10,000 lux, morning, duration)
- Partner accountability structure (what research says works vs. fails)
- Supplement evidence (creatine, fish oil, D3+K2 — what's real, what's marketing)
- Crisis/mental health referrals (when the practice isn't enough)
