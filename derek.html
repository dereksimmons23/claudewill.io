Derek Claude Simmons
53. Minnesota. Coach.

I’m a visual journalist. First and foremost. Thirty years building visual storytelling systems at the Los Angeles Times and the Star Tribune. Basketball coach. Writer. Builder. Father of two boys—Jackson plays college basketball, Teegan’s in middle school.

My middle name is Claude. Hidden on mastheads for years. Four generations carry it: Claude William Simmons, born in Oklahoma Territory in 1903. Claude William Jr. Derek Claude. Jackson Claude. CW raised eleven kids through the dust bowls with a sixth-grade education. He worked 51 years. The standard runs on his initials.

the work

Started at the Wichita Eagle. Went to the LA Times as deputy design director. Six years. Top winner worldwide at the Society for News Design for the better part of a decade. 109 awards in a single year. Widest margin between first and second place in the 32-year history of the competition. WWDD bracelets in the newsroom. When I left, Michael and Colin wrote: “He made us better.”

Came back to the Star Tribune. Twenty years, 13 roles, first and only Chief Creative Officer and VP. Built the visual system. Crossed from editorial to business. Generated $20M during a pandemic.

In 2018, the Star Tribune published Denied Justice—a nine-part investigation into how Minnesota law enforcement failed sexual assault survivors. I guided it, art directed it, and built the process that produced it. Typography-only cover. Restraint. Renée Jones Schneider’s portraits gave the survivors dignity the system had denied them. Pulitzer finalist. Four state laws changed.

The night Prince died, we published “Purple Majesty” by morning. Over 10 days I art directed a 16-page commemorative section with four unique covers. Commissioned Robert Carter—two original paintings in less than a week. Negotiated the rights to a 1977 Robert Whitman photograph of Prince at 19, before his first record deal. Reprints still sell today, 10 years later.

Position eliminated October 2024. Started building the next day.

the practice

The method hasn’t changed. Someone brings me something, we talk through it, they go try something, we iterate. Coached designers at the Star Tribune. Coached a newsroom at the LA Times. Now I coach a machine.

I built claudewill.io from scratch using AI as my collaborator—HTML, CSS, vanilla JavaScript, serverless, Anthropic AI. No frameworks. No templates. The code was written in daily conversation with Claude Code, the same way I used to work with designers: here’s what I’m thinking, go try it, let’s iterate. The method didn’t change. The collaborator did.

I run Coach D—basketball training with 157 drills and an AI coaching layer. I consult through CW Strategies. And I write.

the writing

Claude writes essays from inside the machine. I edit. Eleven published—The Loss Function, The Jar, The Disclaimer, The Bright Line, and more. Not about AI. About what happens when a human in crisis starts talking to a language model and doesn’t stop.

I write dispatches from inside the fire on Substack. The daily practice, the method, the rebuilding year.

The book is called Claude Will. Two words, capitalized, period. A sentence—subject, verb. The name that started in Oklahoma Territory in 1903 and kept going. Deadline: June 28, 2026.

Somebody should be writing this down while it’s happening.
