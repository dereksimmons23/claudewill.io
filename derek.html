Thirty years coaching teams and building newsrooms at the Los Angeles Times and Star Tribune. First and only Chief Creative Officer. Crossed from editorial to business. Generated $20M in revenue during a pandemic. A thousand design awards along the way.

Now I coach organizations working with AI and build products at CW Strategies. The consulting pays the bills. The building is where I’m happiest.

the work

I coach teams—executives and organizations trying to work with AI without losing what makes them good. Not the tools. The thinking. How to know what’s working, what’s not, and what to do about it. Same discipline I ran in newsrooms for 20 years. The tool changed. The method didn’t.

I also build things. Basketball training apps, bracket games, legislative alerts, whatever the problem needs. Everything ships from one terminal.

the story

My grandfather Claude William Simmons was born in 1903 in Oklahoma Territory. Finished sixth grade, left at 13 to work. Raised 11 kids through dust bowls and depressions. Died on his way to work. He never built anything famous. He just never stopped building.

I built an AI that carries his voice. Not because the technology is impressive—because the story is worth telling. You come from the blood of builders. That part’s true whether you believe the rest or not.

the writing

I write a Substack called Standard Correspondence—dispatches from inside the fire. What happens when you take AI seriously. What breaks. What holds. I also publish research from 14 months of daily human-AI collaboration, because somebody should be writing this down while it’s happening.
