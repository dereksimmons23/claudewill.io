Who I am

Thirty years in newsrooms. Fourteen months building what comes next.

I spent 30 years in newsrooms — the Associated Press, the Los Angeles Times, and the Star Tribune, where I became the first Chief Creative Officer in its 150-year history. My position was eliminated in 2024.

Since then I've spent 14 months building a working practice with AI — films, a book, software, a method — every day, in public, at claudewill.io. Not AI as a feature bolted onto the old work. AI as the way the work gets made.

The writing is Being Claude and the book. The film is below. The method is the thing underneath all of it — and it's the thing I'd bring anywhere.

LIGHTNING/bug

The work now

A 5:47 film about two kinds of light — the lightning and the firefly. The first film from sssstudios, finished as a Mother's Day release in May 2026.

Approach

Made entirely from a terminal — generated frames, API calls, and ffmpeg. No camera, no crew, no set. It's the same working method behind everything at claudewill.io, pointed at a film instead of a page: the human decides what it's about and what's true; the machine does the rendering.

Purple Majesty: Remembering a
Minnesota Icon

The work before

Prince died on a Thursday. Every paper in the country would run it. Minnesota's paper — his home state — had to own it. We did.

Approach

By Friday morning we published "Purple Majesty." Over the next 10 days I art directed a 16-page commemorative section with four unique zoned covers. Commissioned two original paintings from Robert Carter in less than a week. Negotiated rights to a rarely seen 1977 photograph taken when Prince was 19.

The name

Claude William Simmons, 1903–1967.

Oklahoma farmer. Builder. Patriarch. Born in Oklahoma Territory before it was a state. Raised his family through the Depression. Fixed what was broken, built what was needed, helped whoever showed up. Never wrote any of it down.

The practice carries his name because it carries his instinct: show up, do the work, tell the truth, help the person in front of you. Claude is also my middle name. The asterisk in derek*claude is where the two meet.
