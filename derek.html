Prince

Commemorative · Star Tribune · April 2016 · 16-page Section

Scroll → A1, four zoned covers, interior pages

Prince died on Thursday. By Friday morning we published “Purple Majesty.” Over the next 10 days I art directed a 16-page commemorative section with four unique zoned covers, commissioned two original paintings from Robert Carter in under a week, and negotiated rights to a rarely seen 1977 photograph taken when Prince was 19.

Every paper in the country ran Prince coverage. The editorial decisions — when to use illustration vs. photography, how to hold the tone of a state’s mourning — those are the backstory. Reprints still sell 10 years later. The section became the proof of concept for Star Tribune Magazine.

“He stands up for his artists. His clear vision and decisiveness was key. Those portraits are still one of my most sought after paintings today.”

— Robert Carter, illustrator

Denied Justice

Investigative · Star Tribune · 2018 · Pulitzer Finalist · SPJ Public Service

Scroll → The special section

More than 1,000 cases. 74% never sent to prosecutors. My role wasn’t to design it — it was to make the case for why this investigation deserved the visual commitment, then guide the process. The typography-only cover, the portrait approach, the restraint — those came out of iteration. Renée Jones Schneider’s photography gave the survivors dignity the system denied them.

Minnesota changed four laws. When George Floyd was murdered in our city, the system we’d built produced the 2021 Pulitzer for Breaking News. I was on the business side by then. I wasn’t in the room. I didn’t need to be.

The Practice

How I Work

The method hasn’t changed in 30 years

Someone brings me something. We talk through it. They go try something. We iterate. Coached designers at the Star Tribune. Coached a newsroom at the LA Times. Now I coach a machine.

I built claudewill.io from scratch using AI as my collaborator — HTML, CSS, vanilla JavaScript, serverless, Anthropic AI. No frameworks. No templates. The code was written in daily conversation with Claude Code, the same way I used to work with designers: here’s what I’m thinking, go try it, let’s iterate. The method didn’t change. The collaborator did.

I run Coach D — basketball training with 157 drills and an AI coaching layer. I consult through CW Strategies. And I write.
